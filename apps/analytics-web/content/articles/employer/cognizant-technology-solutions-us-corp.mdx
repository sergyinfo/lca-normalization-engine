Cognizant Technology Solutions has consistently been the **single largest
H-1B sponsor in the United States** for years. The volume here is not an
artifact of name normalisation &mdash; even after collapsing duplicate
spellings like "Cognizant Technology Solutions Corporation" and the older
"Cognizant Technology Solutions US Corp." into one canonical record, the
firm stands well ahead of the next-largest sponsor.

### Why the volume is so high

Cognizant is a US-headquartered IT services and consulting firm with a
delivery model that relies heavily on H-1B-sponsored engineering and
analyst roles. Most filings cluster in **SOC 15-1252 (Software Developers)**
and adjacent technical occupations. The certification rate is typical for
a large IT-services sponsor &mdash; the bulk of cases come back certified,
with a meaningful share marked *certified-withdrawn* (the case was approved
by DOL but the worker did not ultimately need it, often because the project
or candidate changed).

### What the data does not show

Filing volume is **not the same as new hires**. An H-1B LCA is filed for
each worksite-and-role combination, so a single worker who changes worksite
can generate multiple filings. The numbers here are useful as a *demand
signal* &mdash; they tell you which firms are hiring at scale for which
roles &mdash; but they overstate the count of distinct visa-holding
employees on staff at any given moment.
